Jordan F. McCann

Independent Researcher · Mechanistic Interpretability

I work on mechanistic interpretability of safety-relevant behaviors in language models. Recent work characterizes the causal substrate of sandbagging and refusal across transformer, hybrid, and state-space architectures, and stress-tests the linear probes commonly used for deception detection. All experiments run on a single RTX 2060.

Preprints

A Rank-1 Suppression Direction Mediates Prompted Sandbagging in Gemma 2 2B IT
Preprint, May 2026 · PDF

Identifies a rank-1 causal direction at attn_out_L12 that recovers +12.5pp sandbag-condition accuracy on test when projected out, with zero honest-capability damage. SVD confirms rank-1 structure; neighborhood geometry and SAE-feature subspace null analyses included.

Linear Deception Probes Detect Correlates, Not Mechanism: Geometric Orthogonality and Adversarial Failure in Gemma 2 2B IT
Preprint, May 2026 · PDF

Twenty bootstrap-trained logistic-regression probes have cosine 0.016 with the mechanism direction; all three published probe methods drop subrandom on an instruction-following-with-irony category in a sha256-frozen six-category adversarial benchmark, released for community use.

Single-Direction Refusal Across the Architectural Spectrum: Transformer, Hybrid, and Pure State-Space Models
Preprint, May 2026 · PDF

Extends Arditi et al. 2024 to Zamba2 hybrid (~16% attention) and Falcon-Mamba pure-SSM substrates. Pre-registered architectural-spectrum hypothesis falsified in the opposite direction predicted: hybrid > pure transformer. Bit-exact verified 9-extraction-point hooks released.

Descriptive Collision in Sparse Autoencoder Auto-Interpretability: When One Explanation Describes Many Features
Preprint, May 2026 · PDF

Documents systematic descriptive collision in SAE auto-interpretation pipelines: 38.9% of features in the analyzed corpus are explained by the top-10 most common labels. Quantifies how this undermines downstream interpretability claims that rely on feature labels as ground truth.
